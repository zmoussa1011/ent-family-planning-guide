About the data: Every ACGME-accredited program is bound by the same institutional minimum — at least 6 weeks of paid medical/parental/caregiver leave (100% salary), plus 1 additional paid week, effective since July 2022. That floor is listed for every row. Program- or institution-specific details — extended leave, lactation/childcare support, and fertility or birth-procedure insurance coverage — are rarely published publicly. Where I could verify a specific policy from a program's own materials or a peer-reviewed source, it's cited. Everywhere else is marked Not publicly available — confirm directly with the program's GME office or current residents.

Sources & methodology: Program list compiled from ACGME-accredited otolaryngology–head and neck surgery sponsoring institutions and public residency directories (ACGME Program Finder, AMA FREIDA, SF Match/Otomatch, institutional program pages). The 6-week paid leave floor reflects ACGME Institutional Requirement IV.H (now Section 4.8), effective July 1, 2022, enforced from July 1, 2023 — ACGME Answers: Resident Leave Policies. Published research (e.g. The American Journal of Medicine, 2023) and a 2024 study of surgical subspecialty programs both found that most residency programs do not publish specific parental-leave or family-benefit details online beyond the ACGME floor, which is why most program-specific cells here read "Not publicly available."

This is a living reference, not a verified directory — program rosters change yearly and some benefits (fertility coverage in particular) depend on the sponsoring institution's employee health plan, not the ENT department itself. Please verify directly with programs before making decisions, and treat entries as a starting point for questions to ask on the interview trail.
